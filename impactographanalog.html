Impact-O-Graph Analog Recorder

Detects and records all shocks (up to 50g), time and direction of impact on the X, Y, and Z axis lines.

Logs impact events for up to six months with one standard C-Cell battery.

Data is viewable throughout mission.

Additional Information

Application

Mount the Impact Recorder securely to the object to be monitored. It is recommended that the X-axis be mounted along the direction of travel if this is known.

Remove the stylus guard and set the chart paper to the starting time. Turn the recorder on. Lock the cover if desired. The recorder is ready to document impacts on pressure sensitive paper

How To Apply

Note: lOG Recorders are delivered ready to use with paper and battery
already installed (no ink is used). An Operations Manual is provided with
each recorder.

Step 1 - Mount the Impact Recorder securely to the object to be monitored.

Step 2 - Remove the stylus guard and set the chart paper to the starting time.

Step 3 - Turn the recorder on. Lock the cover if desired. The recorder is ready to document impacts on pressure sensitive paper.

How It Works

Charts magnitude, direction and time of impact. Records continuously for up to 6 months. Shocks up to 50 times the force of gravity (50g) can be identified immediately by examining the chart. Three mass-spring styluses work independently of each other, each measuring one axis- X, Y and Z. Each stylus operates by angular vibration with one degree of freedom in the direction it monitors. The mechanical , friction-free linkage from each stylus’inertia mass converts motion from any of the three directions into the same plane and draws these forces onto the chart paper for easy reading.
